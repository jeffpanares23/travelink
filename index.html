HISTORICAL BACKGROUND

“In early times, the place now known as NAGA was abundant with trees locally called “Narra”. The first settlers named the place as “Narra” (derived from the narra trees) and named it in later years to what it is now – NAGA.

  • Considered as one of the oldest settlements in the Province of Cebu, Naga became a municipality on June 12, 1829 and has gone through a series of reclassification simultaneous with its healthy economic development as it grows.
  • The tranquil farming and fishing villages of the natives were raised to a fuss when the Spaniards arrived and explored the coal mining in upland barangays. Coal then became the first export product of the town, as the Spaniards mined and sent them to Spain. Second to coal was cement product.
  • The mining industry undeniably triggered the successful industrial development of Naga. As the years progressed, quite a number of manufacturing companies and other industries flourished in the place and eventually makes Naga as one of the industrialized locality in Cebu at present.

NAGA has recently gained its new status from 1st class municipality into a component city in the First District of Cebu by virtue of R.A. 9491 which was ratified through a plebiscite on September 02, 2007 under the sponsorship of Congressman Eduardo R. Gullas, now respected as the “Father of the City of Naga, Cebu”.

Museo De Naga

"Museo Naga is not just about having a tourist spot in the city but a way for citizens to have an appreciation of history and to have a sense of pride."

Museo Naga is meant to be “a platform where the Nagahanons and the visiting public may appreciate the City of Naga’s history, heritage, environment and its adaptations through time.” It is a special project of Mayor Kristine Vanessa Chiong. It has three exhibit galleries: Natural History, Naga Heritage and Contemporary Gallery, and the Leader’s Gallery, as well as a library and a conference room.

Pasilong sa Naga

Designer Conbonpue described the interlocking rings as representations of the circles of life and nature’s way of taking and giving back to the earth.

Literally means “to find shade in Naga”, Pasilong sa Naga is an architectural park that boasts interlocking rings of elevated walkways, mesmerizing lights, benches, and fountains in the middle of towering acacia trees. Pasilong sa Naga was designed by Kenneth Cobonpue, a world renowned Cebuano designer, also known for designing the famous Sky Park at SM Seaside Cebu.

Boardwalk

Nothing is more soothing than taking in a gorgeous view and a nice breeze on a sunny day in Naga, which is also in the running for greatest spot to unwind.

The Boardwalk in Naga City offers a spacious, open environment that is suitable to relaxation and provides a broad view of the sea and beautiful mountains. This wide recreation gained the title as the most well-liked public park in the City of Naga. One of the numerous simple items to do in Cebu is to visit this site, and the greatest part is that there is no entrance fees.

Manayon's Peak

The greatest escape from stress comes at the top. Having a vast spectacular view, unpolluted air and nature’s sound calms us like no other, which is what this new spot from Naga flaunts and more.

Located in Naga, a few kilometers away from the national highway, is a breathtaking mountain of green, Manayon’s Peak. Here you can watch the sunset with your friends and might set up camp for some star gazing.

Sayaw River

Nature has its own way of entertaining people. Beaches flaunt wide stretches of white sand shorelines. Waterfalls marvel their spectators with their forceful water drops from towering heights.

The newest attraction in City of Naga mesmerizes visitors with water flows that appear to be moving to the beat of the world of nature. Sayaw River in Lutac, Naga City offers a welcome break. It is exciting to hear the little water flows gently colliding with the river's surface. It is mentally uplifting and soothing.

Mount Naupa

Steep mountains and ranges with an elevation of more than 2,500 meters above sea level hold in its peaks a natural phenomenon that many travelers and mountain hikers look forward to – the sea of clouds.

Mt. Naupa is a mountain with an almost-perfect cone and stands as the highest peak in Naga. It stands at only around 587 meters above sea level. Despite being barely 1000 meters above sea level, the mountain occasionally has a sea of clouds. With Mt. Naupa being one of the most accessible mountains in Cebu, this also makes it the most accessible viewing point to witness a sea of clouds.

Dagitab Festival

Festival

Dagitab Festival was declared as the official cultural festival name of the City of Naga.

  • It is the official grand festival of lights of the City of Naga, Cebu celebrated every December 23. It is identified as one of the city’s flagship destinations.
  • Dagitab is a Cebuano term, which means light. It covers six aspects of the City of Naga, namely: light, progress, industry, tourism, economic development and employment.
  • It involves myriad shows of lights and colors. From the traditional way, it now follows the contemporary style which symbolizes the transformation of Naga from a laidback town to a vibrant and progressive city.

"The presence of different industrial companies which zoom Naga to progress giving hope to all Nagahanons for blissful lives!"

Sugat sa Naga

Traditional Performance

It is a traditional performance every Easter Sunday, as part of the Holy Week celebration.

  • Before the traditional meeting of the image of the resurrected Jesus and the Virgin Mary, there is also a performance drama about the life of Jesus, from his betrayal to his crucifixion until his death and resurrection.
  • It is estimated that the show or drama started in the 1950's under the supervision of the church. In order to preserve the tradition, the management of the festival was transferred to the local government of Naga in the year 2000. Since that transfer until 2016, the employees of the local government of Naga have acted as dramatists (artists) in the Wounded.
  • Coming in 2017 under the administration of Mayor Kristine and now in 2018, the mayor gave the role to the youth as dramatists or artists in Sugat.

This is to involve the youth and pass on to them the preservation of that tradition.
